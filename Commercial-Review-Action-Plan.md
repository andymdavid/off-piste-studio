# Commercial Review Action Plan

Living working document for the Off Piste Studio homepage, service model, pricing model, and broader commercial site direction.

## Current Direction

The proposed `/duplicate-home` direction is preferred as the future visual and structural direction, but the commercial model needs to become clearer before it replaces the current homepage.

The core offer should stay simple:

> Businesses need websites. Off Piste builds them.

The opportunity is to explain why a modern website now has more jobs to do: it needs to help a business get found, understood, trusted, and contacted across Google, Maps, referrals, comparison research, and AI-assisted discovery.

## Agreed Principles

- Keep the homepage website-first and commercially clear.
- Do not force abstract strategy language into the hero.
- Keep the current `/duplicate-home` hero direction for now:
  - "Websites that work as hard as you."
  - "We craft AI-enabled websites, brands and content that turn your expertise into growth."
- Treat "digital authority system" as internal strategy language, not front-facing hero copy.
- Frame AI practically, not conceptually.
- Avoid making "AI-enabled" the main value proposition unless the site explains exactly what that means.
- Separate operational support/hosting from true growth retainers.
- Use service pages and an Approach page for deeper explanation.

## Commercial Model

The current service framing uses:

- Identity
- Conversion
- Customer Experience
- Content

This should be evolved into a tighter commercial model that is easier for buyers to understand.

### Proposed 4-Part Model

#### Brand

Positioning, messaging, identity, and collateral that make the business easier to understand and trust.

#### Website

Fast, professional websites built to explain the offer, convert visitors, and support the way customers actually buy.

#### Search

SEO, local authority, structured content, service pages, location pages, Google Business Profile, and AI-assisted discoverability.

#### Growth

Ongoing content, reporting, updates, conversion improvements, and performance iteration after launch.

### Optional 5-Part Model

If conversion needs to stand alone:

- Brand
- Website
- Search
- Conversion
- Growth

Current recommendation: start with the 4-part model. Conversion can live inside Website and Growth.

## Homepage Action Plan

### 1. Keep The Hero Stable

Treat the current `/duplicate-home` hero as directionally approved for now.

Tasks:

- Keep the website-first framing.
- Avoid adding abstract AI/digital authority language to the hero.
- Revisit only after the surrounding commercial model is clearer.

### 2. Condense The Service Model

Replace or evolve the current `Identity / Conversion / Customer Experience / Content` section into the 4-part model:

- Brand
- Website
- Search
- Growth

The goal is to present a commercial system, not just a capability list.

### 3. Add Homepage Pathways

Add a homepage section that helps visitors self-select without turning the homepage into the pricing page.

Working section title:

> Choose the right starting point

Suggested pathways:

#### Website Build

For businesses that need a professional website launched properly.

#### Website + Search

For businesses that need a website plus SEO foundations, local pages, and stronger discovery.

#### Growth Partnership

For businesses that want ongoing content, SEO, updates, reporting, and performance improvement.

Initial links can go to pricing/contact. Later they can link to dedicated service pages.

### 4. Separate Hosting From Growth

Hosting and support are operational. Growth is commercial and strategic.

Recommended model:

#### Managed Hosting

Hosting, SSL, backups, uptime monitoring, security, and small edits.

#### Managed Support

Hosting plus more frequent updates and priority support.

#### Growth Partnership

SEO/content iteration, new pages, reporting, conversion improvements, AI/search readiness, analytics, and campaign support.

Key decision:

- Stop using "Growth" for the `$99/mo` support plan. It weakens the perceived value of the actual growth offer.

### 5. Create Or Refine Service Pages

The deeper commercial and AI/search language likely belongs on dedicated service pages rather than being overloaded on the homepage.

Likely page set:

- `/services/branding`
- `/services/website-design`
- `/services/search`
- `/services/growth-partnership`

Potential decision:

- Keep `/services/seo`, but broaden or reposition it as Search if that becomes the preferred structure.

Each service page should cover:

- Clear problem
- Who it is for
- What is included
- Why it matters now
- Proof/examples
- CTA
- FAQ

### 6. Create An Approach Page

Create a dedicated `/approach` page for the bigger argument.

Purpose:

- Explain how Off Piste thinks about modern websites without overloading the homepage.

Suggested sections:

- Why websites still matter
- How discovery is changing: Google, Maps, AI answers, referrals, comparison research
- What a modern website needs to do
- The Off Piste process
- The 4-part model: Brand, Website, Search, Growth
- What happens after launch
- FAQs

### 7. Update Homepage Proof

Improve the selected works section so it demonstrates commercial value, not just visual taste.

For each case, aim to add:

- Client name
- Industry
- What changed
- Commercial job of the project
- Link to case study or work page

Even without hard metrics, use outcome language:

- clarified a complex offer
- supported capital raising
- improved enquiry quality
- created a sharper local presence
- helped explain a technical service more clearly

### 8. Revise Homepage FAQ

Replace generic operational FAQs with buyer objections.

Better homepage FAQ topics:

- Do I just need a website, or do I need SEO too?
- How do you make a website easier to find?
- What does AI change about websites?
- Can you support the site after launch?
- How much should a small business website cost?
- How long does a project take?

Keep refund/revision details for pricing or terms, not the homepage.

### 9. Review Pricing

Align pricing with the new commercial model.

Tasks:

- Rename `$99/mo Managed + Growth` to avoid devaluing growth.
- Keep website packages clear.
- Add a separate Growth Partnership section.
- Decide whether Growth Partnership has a starting price or is quote-based.
- Ensure pricing links back to the three homepage pathways.

### 10. SEO And Information Architecture Cleanup

Once the commercial structure is settled:

- Decide final service URLs.
- Update nav/footer.
- Add `/approach`.
- Update internal links.
- Update metadata.
- Add schema where appropriate.
- Ensure `/duplicate-home` direction can become `/` cleanly.

4. Revise pricing architecture.
5. Create `/approach`.
6. Update proof/case study language.
7. Update FAQ.

9. Review visually and commercially.
10. Launch the new homepage direction.

## Open Decisions

- Should Search replace SEO as the service category name?
- Should `/services/seo` remain, redirect, or become `/services/search`?
- Should Growth Partnership show a starting price or stay quote-based?
- Should the homepage include a short AI/search section, or should that mostly live on `/approach`?
- Should the service model be 4 parts or 5 parts?
- Which case studies can credibly include stronger outcome language?

## Notes

### Services List Direction

Existing `/duplicate-home` layout should be preserved:

- Large category word on the left.
- Short capability list on the right.
- Four horizontal rows.
- No major layout change required.

Current service labels:

- Identity
- Conversion
- Customer Experience
- Content

Preferred direction is to update the category language while keeping the same visual structure.

Working recommendation:

- Brand
- Website
- Search
- Growth

Rationale:

- "Brand" is more familiar than "Positioning" and can still include positioning, messaging, identity, and collateral.
- "Website" should absorb conversion paths because the website is the primary commercial asset being sold.
- "Search" should combine SEO, local authority, structured content, and AI-assisted discovery.
- "Growth" should cover ongoing content, reporting, optimisation, updates, and retained improvement after launch.

Alternative if conversion needs to remain explicit:

- Brand
- Website
- Search
- Conversion
- Growth

Current recommendation remains the 4-part model because it is cleaner and works with the existing four-row layout.
