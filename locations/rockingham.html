Rockingham's local business landscape

Rockingham sits on the coast south of Perth and has seen significant population growth over the past decade. The area has a strong community identity and a local economy driven by trades, service businesses, retail, and hospitality.

The Rockingham foreshore and surrounding commercial areas are busy with cafes, restaurants, and retail outlets. Inland, the suburbs of Baldivis, Warnbro, and Safety Bay are home to a large population of families — and the trades and services businesses that support them.

New housing developments across the southern corridor mean builders, plumbers, electricians, and landscapers are in constant demand. But competition is growing too, and a professional online presence is what separates the businesses that get steady work from those that rely on luck.

Coastal tourism and the Shoalwater marine precinct

Rockingham is not just a residential suburb — it has a genuine tourism economy. The Shoalwater Islands Marine Park, Penguin Island, and the sheltered beaches along the foreshore attract visitors from across the Perth metro and beyond. Dolphin and sea lion tours operate year-round, while the foreshore boardwalk and surrounding cafes create a seaside atmosphere that drives hospitality trade.

For tourism and hospitality businesses in Rockingham, your website is how visitors decide whether to book. Tour operators need online booking, restaurants need menus and location details that load instantly on mobile, and accommodation providers need imagery and reviews that build confidence.

A growing population hub

The City of Rockingham is one of the largest local government areas in WA by population, and the growth shows no signs of slowing. Baldivis alone has grown from a semi-rural area to one of Perth's most populated suburbs in under two decades. New estates continue to open, and with them comes demand for every type of local service.

This population growth has also attracted new commercial investment. The Rockingham Shopping Centre has expanded, new medical centres have opened, and the foreshore precinct has been upgraded with public infrastructure to support increased foot traffic.
