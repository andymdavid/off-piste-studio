## Midland's commercial landscape

Midland has undergone significant redevelopment in recent years. The Midland Gate Shopping Centre anchors the retail precinct, while the surrounding area has attracted new commercial investment, health services, and government offices. St John of God Midland Public Hospital has brought a wave of health-related businesses to the area.

The eastern suburbs have always had a strong trades presence. Builders, plumbers, electricians, and landscapers operate throughout Midland, Guildford, Woodbridge, and into the Perth Hills. These businesses depend on local customers finding them when they need work done.

As Perth's urban sprawl pushes east and north-east — with Ellenbrook and The Vines bringing thousands of new residents — the demand for local services in the Midland region continues to grow.

### Midland Gate and the eastern retail precinct

Midland Gate is the largest shopping centre in the eastern suburbs and serves as the retail anchor for a wide catchment. The centre draws customers from as far as the Perth Hills, the Swan Valley, and the newer suburbs of Ellenbrook and Brabham. The surrounding commercial precinct includes fast food outlets, service businesses, and a growing number of medical and professional offices.

The Midland town centre itself has benefited from government investment, including the relocation of state government offices and the development of the Midland Oval precinct. These changes are gradually shifting Midland from a pass-through town to a destination with its own commercial identity.

### Eastern gateway and rail hub

Midland sits at the end of the Midland rail line, making it one of the most accessible eastern suburbs by public transport. The train station connects Midland directly to the Perth CBD, and the surrounding bus network extends into the hills and northern suburbs.

This transport connectivity makes Midland a natural hub for businesses that serve a wide catchment. Trades and professional services based here can reach customers across the eastern suburbs, into the hills, and through to Ellenbrook and beyond. The rail connection also brings workers and shoppers into Midland from surrounding areas, supporting the local retail and hospitality economy.

### Swan Valley proximity

Midland sits at the gateway to the Swan Valley, one of Perth's premier food and wine regions. Wineries, restaurants, breweries, and tourism operators in the Swan Valley benefit from the proximity to Midland's commercial infrastructure. Many Swan Valley businesses use Midland as their service and supply base.

The tourism overflow from the Swan Valley also benefits Midland businesses. Visitors passing through on their way to the valley stop for fuel, food, and supplies. Accommodation providers in the area cater to tourists who want to be close to the valley without paying premium prices.
