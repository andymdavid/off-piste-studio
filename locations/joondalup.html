Joondalup's growing business scene

Joondalup has transformed from a suburban centre into a genuine commercial hub for Perth's northern corridor. The area around Lakeside Shopping City draws significant foot traffic, while the Joondalup Health Campus has created a thriving health precinct with medical clinics, specialists, and allied health businesses clustered nearby.

Beyond the major centres, Joondalup and surrounding suburbs like Currambine, Kinross, and Edgewater are home to a broad mix of trades, professional services, and retail businesses — all serving a large and growing population in the northern suburbs.

For businesses in this area, a professional website is not optional. Your customers are searching online, and if your site does not perform, they will find a competitor who does.

Lakeside Shopping City and the Joondalup CBD

Lakeside Shopping City is the commercial anchor of the northern corridor. With major retailers, specialty stores, and a cinema complex, it draws shoppers from suburbs stretching from Clarkson to Hillarys. The surrounding Joondalup city centre includes government offices, a police academy, and a growing number of professional service firms that have set up here rather than commute to the Perth CBD.

The City of Joondalup has actively developed the area as a secondary city centre, and the result is a business environment that increasingly competes with traditional inner-city locations for professional tenants and customers.

The ECU campus and health precinct

Edith Cowan University's Joondalup campus brings thousands of students and staff to the area, creating a built-in customer base for food, retail, and service businesses nearby. The campus also feeds a pipeline of graduates into local businesses, particularly in health, education, and IT.

The Joondalup Health Campus, operated by Ramsay Health Care, is one of the largest hospitals in the northern corridor. The medical precinct surrounding it includes GP clinics, physiotherapists, psychologists, dentists, pathology labs, and specialist consulting rooms. These health businesses depend heavily on local search traffic — patients search for practitioners near them, and the businesses with strong websites and Google profiles capture the bulk of those bookings.
