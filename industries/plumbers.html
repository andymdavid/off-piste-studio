Website Design for Plumbers — More Leads for Your Plumbing Business

Plumbing websites need to do two completely different jobs at once — catch the panicked homeowner with water pouring across their kitchen floor, and attract the couple planning a bathroom renovation who will spend weeks comparing options. We build plumbing websites that handle both, with distinct user journeys that convert each type of visitor into a lead.

Two audiences, two journeys, one website

A plumbing website has a split personality problem that most web designers ignore. Half your visitors are in crisis — a burst pipe, a blocked sewer, a gas smell in the kitchen. They need to call you in under ten seconds. The other half are planning something — a bathroom renovation, a hot water system replacement, a new home fit-out. They want information, images, and reassurance before they even think about picking up the phone.

We design plumbing websites with two distinct user pathways built into the same site. The emergency pathway features a sticky call bar that stays visible on every page, simplified page layouts that load in under two seconds on mobile, and one-tap contact that works without scrolling. The planned work pathway is richer — detailed service pages, quote request forms with fields for project scope, before-and-after galleries, and content that helps the customer understand what their project involves before they make contact. These are not two separate websites. They are two experiences woven into a single design, and the visitor naturally lands in the right one based on how they arrived and what they are looking for.

Gas fitting deserves its own page

Most plumbing websites list gas fitting as a bullet point under "Our Services" and leave it at that. This is a missed opportunity. Gas fitting is a premium, high-trust service that requires separate licensing in every Australian state. Customers searching for gas fitting work — whether it is a gas leak repair, a cooktop installation, or a gas hot water connection — are specifically looking for a licensed gas fitter, not a general plumber.

We build dedicated gas fitting pages that display your gas fitting licence number separately from your plumbing licence. The distinction matters. A homeowner who smells gas does not want to see "we also do gas work" buried in a list. They want to see a gas fitting licence number, clear language about gas leak response, and a phone number they can call immediately. For less urgent gas work — appliance connections, gas line extensions for outdoor kitchens, gas heating installations — the same page provides the detail and credibility that a single bullet point never could. Gas fitting pages consistently rank well because most plumbers neglect them, leaving a gap in the search results that a well-built page fills easily.

A plumber in Adelaide had been listing gas fitting as one line on his services page for three years. We built a dedicated gas fitting page covering gas leak response, appliance installations, and gas compliance certificates with his gas fitting licence number displayed prominently. Within eight weeks it was ranking on page one for "gas fitter Adelaide" and generating four to five qualified gas fitting enquiries per month — work he was already equipped to do but was not attracting online.

Bathroom renovations and the power of before-and-after content

Bathroom renovations represent the highest-value residential plumbing work most plumbers will do. A typical bathroom renovation involves thousands of dollars in plumbing labour alone, yet most plumbing websites treat bathroom work the same as fixing a leaking tap. The customers planning bathroom renovations are a completely different audience. They are browsing Pinterest, saving inspiration images, and comparing multiple tradespeople. They will spend days or weeks deciding who to contact.

We build bathroom renovation sections with before-and-after galleries that showcase your completed work. High-quality images of tiled showers, freestanding baths, and vanity installations communicate craftsmanship in a way that text never can. Each project includes a brief description of the scope — what was removed, what was installed, any complications that were handled — so prospective clients understand the depth of work involved. This type of content attracts a customer who values quality and expects to pay for it, which filters out the price-shoppers and brings in higher-margin work.

Speaking to property managers and commercial clients

Backflow prevention testing, TMV servicing, stormwater compliance, and scheduled maintenance contracts are not the kind of work that generates excited social media posts. But for plumbers who service commercial properties, strata buildings, and property management portfolios, this is steady, recurring revenue that comes from a small number of high-value clients.

The problem is that property managers and body corporates evaluate contractors differently from homeowners. They want evidence of compliance capability, not a friendly photo of you next to your van. We build commercial-focused sections of your plumbing website that speak directly to this audience — backflow testing and certification services, preventative maintenance schedules, compliance documentation, and the capacity to handle multi-unit properties. Your plumbing licence number, ABN, public liability and professional indemnity insurance details appear in context alongside these services, because property managers check. If you hold specific endorsements for thermostatic mixing valve servicing or fire services plumbing, we give those credentials their own space rather than lumping them into a general qualifications list.

Hot water system comparisons as a conversion tool

When a hot water system fails, the homeowner faces a decision they have not thought about in ten or fifteen years. Electric storage, gas continuous flow, heat pump, or solar — the options have changed since their last system was installed, and they have no idea what to choose. They search, they read, they compare. This research phase is an enormous opportunity that most plumbing websites completely miss.

We build hot water system comparison pages that walk the customer through each option — upfront cost, running costs, energy efficiency ratings, expected lifespan, suitability for different household sizes, and available government rebates. These pages rank well because they match a high-intent informational search, and they convert well because the customer finishes reading with enough knowledge to make a decision and enough trust in your expertise to call you rather than someone else. A comparison page that honestly explains when a heat pump makes sense and when it does not positions you as the plumber who gives straight advice, which is exactly what customers are looking for when they are about to spend two to four thousand dollars.
