# Territory Map: Search, AI Discovery, and Findability

Status: Working map for the first topical territory.

Brief: `docs/private/territories/search-ai-discovery/brief.md`

Working frame: being found and trusted in AI-mediated search.

This map turns the territory brief into an operating structure. It assigns existing content, identifies coverage strength, defines internal-linking work, and names the first research priority. It does not list article titles.

## Pillar and Hub Role

This territory should eventually have a dedicated pillar or hub page.

Current state:

- `/resources` is the public insights index, but it is not a strategic hub for this territory.
- `/services/seo` is a service page and carries commercial intent, but it is too narrow to hold the full territory.
- `building-your-website-for-llms` and `google-sge-and-seo` are useful early anchors, but they are individual articles rather than a complete pillar.

Recommended near-term approach:

- Use existing posts as interim anchors.
- Strengthen internal links between the AI, SEO, local search, accessibility, and performance posts.
- Build the territory through cluster depth before creating a formal pillar.
- Revisit the pillar once the first few gaps are filled and the keyword research shows clearer demand patterns.

Future pillar role:

- Explain how modern businesses are found, understood, cited, and trusted across Google, local search, AI search, and answer engines.
- Act as the navigation hub for subclusters.
- Link to commercial pages where relevant, especially `/services/seo` and `/services/website-design`.
- Carry the strongest evidence, diagrams, and frameworks for this territory.

## First Research Priority

First cluster to research deeply: AI Search Visibility.

Reason:

- It is timely.
- It aligns with Off Piste's newer positioning.
- It connects to existing content.
- It has strong official source material.
- It creates natural bridges into schema, structured content, evidence-led content, and measurement.
- It can produce useful visuals and frameworks.

Primary research questions:

- How do AI search tools discover and cite websites?
- What is the practical difference between AI search crawlers and AI training crawlers?
- What should businesses allow, block, or structure for AI discovery?
- What do Google, OpenAI, and Perplexity say officially?
- How do llms.txt, llms-full.txt, schema, robots.txt, and clean HTML fit together?
- What does a business need to provide so AI systems can represent it accurately?

Research source starting set:

- Google AI features guidance.
- Google generative AI Search guidance.
- OpenAI crawler documentation.
- OpenAI publisher and developer FAQ.
- Perplexity crawler documentation.
- Cloudflare AI Crawl Control documentation.
- Google structured data documentation.
- Existing Off Piste `llms.txt`, `llms-full.txt`, and `robots.txt`.

Likely visual needs:

- AI crawler access decision matrix.
- Machine-readable content stack.
- AI search visibility system map.

## First Keyword Research Scope

The first keyword research pass should focus on language around AI search visibility.

Seed concepts:

- AI search optimisation.
- AI search visibility.
- ChatGPT search visibility.
- Perplexity SEO.
- AI Overviews SEO.
- llms.txt.
- AI crawler robots.txt.
- how to get cited by AI search.
- how AI search finds websites.
- website for AI search.
- answer engine optimisation.

Research tasks:

1. Expand seeds through autocomplete, People Also Ask, related searches, and SEO tooling.
2. Classify intent.
3. Identify which queries are business-owner language and which are SEO-practitioner language.
4. Check SERPs for AI Overviews and forum-heavy results.
5. Identify commercial relevance to Off Piste.
6. Identify evidence needed for each major intent group.
7. Decide whether the first output should be a new article, a refresh to `building-your-website-for-llms`, or a future hub section.
