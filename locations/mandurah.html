The sea change demographic

Mandurah has long been known as a sea change destination. Retirees and semi-retirees from Perth and interstate have been drawn here by the waterfront lifestyle, affordable housing compared to coastal suburbs closer to the city, and a relaxed pace of life. This demographic shapes the local economy in specific ways.

Health services, aged care providers, and allied health practitioners are in strong demand. Financial planning and wealth management services cater to retirees managing superannuation and investments. Home maintenance and gardening services thrive because many older residents prefer to outsource physical work on their properties.

At the same time, a younger demographic has moved to Mandurah and the surrounding Peel region in recent years. Affordable land in suburbs like Lakelands and Meadow Springs has attracted first-home buyers and young families, adding demand for childcare, education services, and the full range of trades needed to fit out new homes.

Mandurah's business landscape

Mandurah is Western Australia's largest city outside of Perth and has a thriving local economy. The Mandurah estuary and foreshore are the heart of the city's tourism and hospitality sector, drawing visitors year-round for dolphin cruises, waterfront dining, and the famous Mandurah Crab Fest.

Beyond tourism, Mandurah has a growing services sector. The population includes a significant number of retirees alongside young families moving south for affordable housing, creating demand for trades, health services, professional services, and retail.

The Peel region is also a hub for builders and construction businesses, with ongoing residential development in areas like Lakelands, Meadow Springs, and Halls Head. These businesses need websites that convert local searches into real enquiries.

The canal lifestyle and waterfront economy

Mandurah's canal estates are a defining feature of the city. Thousands of homes sit on waterways that connect to the estuary and ocean, and this waterfront lifestyle drives a specific set of businesses. Boat maintenance and marine services, jetty builders, waterfront landscaping, and canal-side property maintenance are all part of the local economy.

The Mandurah Ocean Marina and the Dolphin Quay precinct bring together restaurants, retail, and tourism operators in a waterfront setting. Businesses in this precinct benefit from foot traffic driven by visitors and locals alike, but they still need a strong online presence to capture the tourists who plan their trips via Google.

What is working in the Mandurah market

Tourism-adjacent businesses consistently perform well. Fishing charter operators, dolphin tour companies, and accommodation providers benefit from Mandurah's reputation as a weekend getaway from Perth.
