What is local SEO and why it matters

When someone searches for a service in their area — "plumber near me" or "accountant in Perth" — Google returns a mix of local results, map listings, and organic results. Local SEO is the process of optimising your business to appear in those results.

For small businesses, local SEO is one of the highest-return marketing investments available. Unlike paid advertising, where leads stop the moment you stop spending, SEO builds an asset. A well-optimised website and Google Business Profile continue to bring in leads month after month.

The businesses that show up on the first page of local search results get the vast majority of clicks. If your business is buried on page two or three, most potential customers will never find you. They will call your competitor instead.

Google Business Profile setup and optimisation

Your Google Business Profile is your most important local SEO asset. It is the listing that appears in Google Maps and the local pack — the box of three businesses that shows up at the top of local search results.

We set up and optimise your profile to maximise visibility:

  • Accurate business name, address, and phone number
  • Correct business categories and service areas
  • Detailed business description with relevant keywords
  • High-quality photos of your business, team, and work
  • Service listings with descriptions
  • Regular posts and updates to keep the profile active
  • Review management strategy to build social proof

A fully optimised Google Business Profile can be the difference between appearing in the local pack and being invisible in search results.

On-page SEO

On-page SEO refers to the optimisation of your website itself. This is the foundation that tells Google what your business does, where you operate, and which searches your pages should appear for.

Title tags and meta descriptions

Every page on your website has a title tag and meta description. These are what appear in search results. We write clear, keyword-targeted title tags and compelling meta descriptions that encourage clicks.

Heading structure

Proper heading structure helps both users and search engines understand your content. We organise your pages with a clear hierarchy — one main heading per page, supporting subheadings for each section, and content that flows logically.

Content optimisation

We review and optimise the content on your key pages to ensure it targets the right keywords, answers the questions your customers are asking, and provides enough depth for Google to consider it authoritative. Thin, generic content does not rank. Detailed, relevant content does.

Page speed and technical foundations

Google uses page speed as a ranking factor, particularly on mobile. We ensure your website loads fast, is mobile-friendly, and meets core technical SEO requirements including proper indexing, structured data, and clean URLs.

Local citations and directory listings

A local citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number. Citations on reputable directories help Google verify that your business is legitimate and located where you say it is.

We submit your business to the directories that matter most in Australia:

  • Google Business Profile
  • Apple Maps
  • Bing Places
  • Yellow Pages
  • True Local
  • Yelp Australia
  • Industry-specific directories relevant to your trade or profession

Consistency is critical. If your business name, address, or phone number varies across directories, it confuses Google and weakens your local rankings. We audit existing citations and correct any inconsistencies.

Keyword research for local businesses

Effective local SEO starts with understanding what your potential customers are actually searching for. We conduct keyword research to identify the terms and phrases that are relevant to your business and have sufficient search volume in your area.

This goes beyond the obvious. A plumber might target "plumber Perth," but there are dozens of related searches — "blocked drain Perth," "hot water system repair," "emergency plumber near me" — that each represent a potential customer with a specific need.

We map keywords to pages on your website so each service page targets a distinct set of search terms. This avoids cannibalisation — where multiple pages compete against each other — and ensures your site covers the full range of searches your customers are making.

How SEO connects to your website design

SEO and web design are not separate disciplines. The structure, speed, and content of your website directly impact your search rankings. A beautiful website that is slow, poorly structured, or thin on content will struggle to rank.

When we build websites, SEO is built into the process from day one. Page structure, URL hierarchy, heading levels, image optimisation, and content strategy are all considered during design and development — not bolted on afterwards.

If your current website was not built with SEO in mind, we can audit the site and identify the changes needed to improve its search performance. Sometimes this means optimising existing pages. Other times it means restructuring content or adding new pages to target important keywords.

Ongoing SEO vs one-time setup

A one-time SEO setup gives your business a strong foundation. This includes Google Business Profile optimisation, on-page SEO for your key pages, local citations, and technical fixes. For many small businesses in low-competition markets, this can be enough to start ranking.

However, SEO is a competitive landscape. Your competitors are also working on their rankings. Google regularly updates its algorithm. And the businesses that invest consistently in SEO are the ones that maintain and grow their positions over time.

Our ongoing SEO packages include:

  • Monthly performance reporting with clear metrics
  • Content updates and new page creation
  • Google Business Profile management and posting
  • Review monitoring and response strategy
  • Ongoing keyword tracking and opportunity identification
  • Technical monitoring to catch and fix issues early

The businesses that treat SEO as an ongoing investment — rather than a one-time project — are the ones that build a sustainable pipeline of inbound leads. It is one of the few marketing channels where the return compounds over time.

Common questions

How much do local SEO services cost?

Our one-time local SEO setup starts from $1,500 AUD and includes Google Business Profile optimisation, on-page SEO, and local citations. Ongoing monthly SEO packages start from $500 per month and include content updates, reporting, and continued optimisation.

How long does it take to see results from SEO?

SEO is not instant. Most businesses start seeing improvements in local rankings within 4 to 12 weeks, depending on competition and the current state of their website. Consistent effort over 3 to 6 months delivers the strongest results.

Do I need ongoing SEO or is a one-time setup enough?

A one-time setup gives you a solid foundation — optimised pages, Google Business Profile, and local citations. But SEO is competitive. If your competitors are investing in ongoing SEO and you are not, they will eventually outrank you. Ongoing SEO keeps your content fresh, your rankings stable, and your lead flow growing.

What is a Google Business Profile and why does it matter?

Google Business Profile is the free listing that appears in Google Maps and local search results. For local businesses, it is often the first thing potential customers see. An optimised profile with accurate information, photos, and reviews significantly increases your chances of being found and contacted.

Can you guarantee first page rankings?

No reputable SEO provider can guarantee specific rankings. Google's algorithm considers hundreds of factors, many outside anyone's control. What we can guarantee is that we follow proven best practices, provide transparent reporting, and focus our efforts on the keywords and areas that matter most to your business.

Do you work with businesses outside Perth?

Yes. While many of our clients are Perth-based, local SEO principles apply to any location. We work with small businesses across Australia to improve their visibility in their local area.
