## Storm damage content that ranks before the storm hits

When a hailstorm tears through a suburb, search volume for "roof damage repair" and "emergency roofer near me" spikes tenfold overnight. The businesses that capture those enquiries are the ones whose storm damage pages are already indexed, already ranking, and already answering the questions panicked homeowners are typing into Google at 11pm.

We build dedicated storm damage and emergency repair pages into your website from day one. These pages explain what homeowners should do immediately after roof damage, how to document damage for insurance purposes, and why temporary tarping prevents further interior damage. They rank for searches like "hail damage roof repair [suburb]" and "roof storm damage what to do" long before the next severe weather event. Trying to publish this content after a storm is like trying to build a boat after the flood — your competitors who planned ahead are already fielding calls.

## Navigating insurance claims — the content that converts

Most homeowners have no idea their home insurance covers storm damage repairs, or what the claims process actually involves. This knowledge gap is your biggest conversion opportunity. A clear, detailed page explaining how roof insurance claims work — what the insurer needs, how your inspection report supports the claim, what out-of-pocket costs to expect — removes the friction that stops homeowners from picking up the phone.

We structure this content to guide the homeowner step by step: document the damage, contact your insurer, book a professional roof inspection, get a detailed scope-of-works report, and have the repairs completed by a licensed roofer. When your website positions you as the business that handles the complexity of insurance work, you attract higher-value jobs and customers who are ready to proceed rather than just price-shopping.

> A roofing business on the Gold Coast added a dedicated insurance claims page explaining the process from inspection through to insurer approval. Within two months, they reported that nearly half their new enquiries mentioned the insurance content specifically — homeowners felt confident the business understood the claims process and would handle the paperwork alongside the repairs.

## Material comparison pages that capture research traffic

Homeowners replacing a roof spend weeks researching materials before they ever request a quote. Colorbond vs concrete tile. Terracotta vs metal. Flat roof membranes vs corrugated steel. These comparison searches represent high-intent traffic — people actively planning a roofing project and evaluating their options.

We create detailed material comparison pages that break down durability, cost, weight, aesthetic options, thermal performance, and suitability for different roof pitches and climate zones. Each page positions your business as the knowledgeable expert, not just another contractor listing services. When a homeowner spends ten minutes reading your detailed comparison of Colorbond profiles and finishes, they are far more likely to request a quote from you than from a competitor whose website says nothing more than "we install all roof types."

## Aerial imagery and the roofing portfolio advantage

Roofers have a visual advantage that no other trade can match: drone photography. A completed kitchen renovation looks good in photos, but a new Colorbond roof shot from 30 metres up — showing clean lines, precise flashing, and a perfect colour match to the facade — is genuinely striking. Before-and-after aerials of a full re-roof or restoration tell a story that ground-level photos simply cannot.

We design project galleries that make the most of this perspective. Each project entry can include the drone overview, close-up detail shots of ridgecapping and valley work, and ground-level context showing how the roof integrates with the property. For restoration projects, the before-and-after comparison from the same aerial angle is one of the most compelling visual proof points any trade website can offer. We display your licence number, insurance details, and industry memberships alongside these galleries so the quality of your work and the legitimacy of your business reinforce each other naturally.

## Beyond the roof — structuring services for the full scope of work

Most roofing businesses do far more than roof replacements. Guttering, downpipes, fascia and soffit repairs, roof ventilation, skylights, leak detection, and annual inspection programs all represent distinct service lines with their own search demand. A website that buries these under a single "Services" page misses significant traffic.

We build individual pages for each service you offer, structured around the specific questions customers ask. Someone searching for gutter replacement has different concerns than someone needing a full re-roof — they want to know about materials, capacity for heavy rainfall, leaf guard options, and whether you replace fascia at the same time. A dedicated roof inspection page targeting annual maintenance checks and pre-purchase building inspections captures a completely different audience: property buyers, landlords, and proactive homeowners. Each page works as its own entry point from Google, expanding the number of searches that lead directly to your business.
