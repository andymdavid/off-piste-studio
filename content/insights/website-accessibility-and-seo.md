## Good structure is good discoverability

Search engines rely on a coherent content model. When heading levels are logical, forms are labeled clearly, and interactive elements are explicit, the page becomes easier to parse. That improves the chances of the page being indexed correctly and associated with the right search intent.

The same fixes also improve the experience for real people. Navigation becomes more predictable, content becomes easier to scan, and the next step becomes clearer. In other words, accessibility upgrades often improve usability and visibility at the same time.

> The work that makes a site easier to use usually makes it easier to understand, and that tends to perform better.

## The commercial case is clarity

Accessibility matters ethically and legally, but the commercial case is just as important. If users cannot move through the page confidently, trust drops. If they cannot reach key proof points or CTAs, conversion drops. Those are business issues, not only compliance issues.

That is why accessibility should be treated as part of a performance program. The outcome is not just a cleaner audit score. It is a clearer, stronger, more reliable buying journey.

## Where to start

- Fix heading hierarchy and page structure before redesigning visuals.
- Review button labels, link text, and form labels for clarity.
- Improve contrast, focus states, and keyboard navigation on high-intent pages.
- Write alt text that adds context where the image carries meaning.
